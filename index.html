Welcome.

I am an Evolutionary Biologist currently doing a post-doc at the Technical University of Denmark being advised by Dr. Jakob Hemmer Hansen & Dr. Dorte Bekkevold. We belong to the Population Genetics Section, which is located at the small town of Silkeborg in Jutland. This section is part of DTU Aqua, a well-known institute in the field of aquatic resources. I am hired under the MarGen II Project, and we have been investigating several fish species concerning the evolutinary imprints left on their genomes caused by a recent evolution in Baltic Sea.
Previously, I did my MSc and PhD at the Natural History Museum of Denmark — University of Copenhagen under the supervisions of Prof. Tom Gilbert & Dr. Filipe Vieira.
A morsel about me: I am originally from the Brazilian Northeast, precisely from a city called Natal (it means Christmas in Portuguese). During my years in this tropical town, I had the opportunity to visit unique localities such as the Rocas Atoll — a marvellous place indeed. I stayed in Natal for my initial education, and by 2011 I would obtain a BSc in Biological Science from my Alma Mater — the Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte. In 2012, right before I turned 23, I moved to Denmark to continue with my education in Biology, and I have been living in this Nordic country ever since. Despite my wonders as to what would be the cognitive status of this rocky planet if it did not have that famous scar on its surface, I see myself as a pretty ordinary person. During my free time, I enjoy reading Literature (especially the classics), and watching animes (my forever favourite is Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood).
